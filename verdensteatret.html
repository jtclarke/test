And All the Question Marks Started to Sing

Verdensteatret

dates.

February 24 - 27, 2011 at Dance Theater Workshop:
219 West 19th Street, New York, NY 10011

about.

Norway-based Verdensteatret returns to New York City after their highly acclaimed Concert for Greenland (2005, PS122) and the debut of Louder (2008, PS122). And All the Question Marks Started to Sing stretches the boundaries of live performance. Situated in a tension between theater, visual art, robotics, and sound, this work demonstrates a deep fascination with all kinds of animation-a strange and somewhat miraculous activity of breathing life into dead objects, stiff figures and frozen images. This landscape of kinetic sculptures, literally "played" and manipulated by members of the company, creates a room in constant transformation.

Co-presented with Dance Theater Workshop and Performance Space 122.

the artist.

Verdensteatret was founded in 1986 and has since moved in many different directions. But on one point they stand fast: their connection to the European, and especially the German, theatrical tradition, with Brecht, Walter Benjamin and Heiner Müller as natural references. Following experiments in fields such as visual performance, environmental theatre and text theatre, the group has worked towards a more intermedial form. Today, Verdensteatret consists of video artists, computer animators, sound engineers, musicians, artists and a painter – among others. They develop their work in a ‘flat structure’ – or, as they themselves explain, ‘everyone interferes with everything’. In this way they search for a form that includes many means of expression and despite the fact that it and involves a good dose of quarreling it can be experienced as ‘integrated’. Video pictures are processed with distorting mirrors and human voices are digitalized, synthetic elements are turned into organic ones, and vice versa.
